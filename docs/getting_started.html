Getting Started¶

You can use Django Categories in two ways:

  1. As storage for one tree of categories, e.g.:

    Top Category 1
  Subcategory 1-1
    Subcategory 1-2
      subcategory 1-2-1
Top Category 2
  Subcategory 2-1

  2. As a storage of several trees of categories, e.g.:

    Model 1
  Category 1
    Subcategory 1-1
      Subcategory 1-2
        subcategory 1-2-1
  Category 2
    Subcategory 2-1
Model 2
  Category 3
    Subcategory 3-1
      Subcategory 3-2
        subcategory 3-2-1
  Category 4
    Subcategory 4-1

You can’t do it as both at the same time, though.

Connecting your model with Django-Categories¶

Because there are a few additional methods and attributes that your model needs, you can’t simply create a ForeignKey to Category, even though that is eventually what happens.

You add a many-to-one or many-to-many relationship with Django Categories using the register_fk() and register_m2m() methods respectively. For example, if you added in your models.py:

import categories
categories.register_fk(MyModel)

MyModel would have a ForeignKey named category
